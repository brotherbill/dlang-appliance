# Content Production Policy for the D Learning Appliance  
# Handcrafted. Controlled Pace. Human‑Authored. AI‑Assisted. No Mass Production.

This file defines how curriculum content is created, reviewed, released, and maintained.  
It ensures that content remains coherent, principled, human‑authored, and aligned with the educational mission.

A man’s got to know his limitations.  
This file defines the curriculum’s.

---

## 1. PURPOSE OF THE CONTENT PRODUCTION POLICY
The purpose of this policy is to:

- preserve curriculum quality  
- preserve tone consistency  
- preserve educational integrity  
- prevent mass‑produced content  
- prevent AI‑generated drift  
- ensure controlled, sustainable release pacing  
- ensure human authorship and oversight  

Content must be crafted, not churned.

---

## 2. INITIAL RELEASE SCOPE
The initial release of the D Learning Appliance includes:

- several weeks of fully handcrafted curriculum  
- a complete, coherent starting arc  
- a stable foundation for student progress  
- a predictable learning path  

The initial release is intentionally limited to preserve quality.

---

## 3. CONTROLLED RELEASE PACE RULE
New content is released only at a pace that:

- preserves quality  
- preserves tone  
- preserves coherence  
- preserves support capacity  
- preserves curriculum integrity  
- preserves operator bandwidth  

There is no pressure to “catch up,” “produce more,” or “accelerate output.”

Curriculum grows at the speed of correctness, not the speed of demand.

---

## 4. HUMAN AUTHORSHIP RULE
All curriculum content is:

- human‑authored  
- human‑designed  
- human‑structured  
- human‑approved  

AI may assist, but AI does not author.

The human operator (Brother Bill) is the architect, teacher, and final authority.

---

## 5. AI‑ASSISTED, NOT AI‑GENERATED RULE
AI may be used for:

- idea generation  
- tone control  
- editing  
- organization  
- clarity improvements  
- structural suggestions  

AI may **not** be used for:

- autonomous lesson generation  
- autonomous video scripts  
- autonomous curriculum arcs  
- autonomous pedagogy decisions  
- autonomous student guidance  

AI is a tool, not a teacher.

---

## 6. NO MASS‑PRODUCTION RULE
Curriculum must never be:

- mass‑generated  
- algorithmically produced  
- bulk‑created  
- auto‑expanded  
- outsourced to AI  
- scaled beyond human review  

Every lesson must be handcrafted, reviewed, and approved.

---

## 7. CURRICULUM INTEGRITY RULE
Curriculum must remain:

- coherent  
- consistent  
- tone‑aligned  
- pedagogically sound  
- age‑appropriate  
- mission‑aligned  
- free of drift  

If a lesson does not meet these standards, it is revised or removed.

---

## 8. SUPPORT‑CAPACITY ALIGNMENT RULE
Content production must align with:

- support capacity  
- student feedback  
- curriculum stability  
- Golden Master stability  
- RCA stability  

If support capacity is strained, content production slows.

---

## 9. NO DRIFT RULE
Content production policy must remain consistent across:

- dlang-appliance  
- dlang-school  
- from-dead-box-to-dev-box  

If one changes, all must change.

---

## 10. REPOSITORY PRIVACY AND OWNERSHIP RULE
dlang-appliance and its curriculum are private, not for sale, and must remain under the sole control of Brother Bill to preserve their integrity and moral purpose.
