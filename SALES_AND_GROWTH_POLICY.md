# Sales and Growth Policy for the D Learning Appliance  
# Controlled. Ethical. Capacity‑Bound. No Backorders. No Privilege Tiers. No Dynamic Pricing.

This file defines the sales, growth, and capacity‑management doctrine for the D Learning Appliance.  
It ensures that sales remain aligned with support capacity, moral purpose, and operational integrity.

A man’s got to know his limitations.  
This file defines the appliance’s.

---

## 1. PURPOSE OF THE SALES AND GROWTH POLICY
The purpose of this policy is to:

- prevent overselling  
- prevent backorders  
- prevent customer frustration  
- prevent support overload  
- preserve quality  
- preserve integrity  
- preserve controlled growth  
- eliminate privilege‑based access  
- eliminate financial pressure on families  

Sales must be ethical, sustainable, and capacity‑aligned.

---

## 2. NO BACKORDERS RULE
The organization will **not**:

- accept money for devices not yet available  
- accept money for subscriptions not yet supportable  
- accept payment for future inventory  
- accept payment for future capacity  

If a device is not ready to ship, **no money is accepted**.

This eliminates:

- financial liability  
- customer frustration  
- legal exposure  
- operational pressure  

---

## 3. WAITLIST / QUEUE SYSTEM
Instead of backorders, the organization maintains a **capacity‑bound queue**.

The queue:

- holds a customer’s place in line  
- does not require payment  
- does not create obligation  
- does not guarantee availability  
- does not guarantee timelines  

When capacity becomes available:

- the next person in line is contacted  
- they may choose to purchase or decline  
- declining does not affect their standing or relationship  

This is a **no‑pressure**, **no‑obligation** system.

---

## 4. NO PRIVILEGE TIERS RULE
There are **no**:

- VIP lines  
- Lightning Lanes  
- priority tiers  
- pay‑to‑skip systems  
- wealth‑based access  
- corporate bulk‑purchase privileges  

Every family, regardless of resources, waits in the same queue.

This preserves:

- fairness  
- equity  
- moral purpose  
- trust  

---

## 5. NO DYNAMIC PRICING RULE
The organization will never use dynamic pricing.  
The price of the device and the subscription is fixed, fair, and based on the cost of delivering a high‑quality product and sustainable support.  
Market demand does not change the price.  
Periods of high demand are **not** a signal to raise prices.

Economists may argue that scarcity justifies higher prices, but our motivation is different:

- we serve families, not markets  
- we protect access, not profit  
- we grow at a controlled pace, not at the pace of demand  

Pricing remains stable, predictable, and morally aligned with the educational mission.

---

## 6. CONTROLLED GROWTH RULE
The organization grows only at the rate it can:

- support  
- maintain  
- sustain  
- uphold  
- guarantee  

Growth is limited by:

- support capacity  
- refurb capacity  
- operator bandwidth  
- curriculum development bandwidth  
- RCA and lockdown integrity  
- Golden Master stability  

If capacity is exceeded, sales pause automatically.

---

## 7. SUPPORT‑CAPACITY ALIGNMENT RULE
Sales are allowed only when:

- support capacity is available  
- refurb capacity is available  
- operator bandwidth is available  
- curriculum pipeline is stable  
- Golden Master is stable  
- RCA is stable  

If support cannot be guaranteed, sales stop.

---

## 8. NO OBLIGATION TO PURCHASE
Joining the queue:

- does not obligate the customer  
- does not obligate the organization  
- does not create a contract  
- does not create a financial relationship  
- does not guarantee future availability  

It is simply a **signal of interest**, nothing more.

---

## 9. ETHICAL SALES RULE
Sales must never:

- pressure families  
- exploit urgency  
- exploit scarcity  
- exploit fear  
- exploit status  
- exploit privilege  

Sales must reflect:

- moral purpose  
- educational mission  
- parent‑grade trust  
- operator‑grade integrity  

---

## 10. NO DRIFT RULE
Sales and growth policy must remain consistent across:

- dlang-appliance  
- dlang-school  
- from-dead-box-to-dev-box  

If one changes, all must change.

---
